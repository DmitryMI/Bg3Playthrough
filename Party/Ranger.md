# Ranger - Beast Master

## Reference

> This build is a mix of  [Ranger Beast Master Build](https://gamestegy.com/post/bg3/1008/ranger-beast-master-build) and [Hunter Ranger Build](https://gamestegy.com/post/bg3/1607/hunter-ranger-archer-build), combining ranged combat with summons.

## Race

Pick any. Consult with reference build if you want the "perfect" option, but this is not required.

## Level 1

Favoured Enemy: [Bounty Hunter](https://bg3.wiki/wiki/Bounty_Hunter) - buffs [Ensnaring Strike](https://bg3.wiki/wiki/Ensnaring_Strike) which synergizes with melee allies.

Natural Explorer: [Beast Tamer](https://bg3.wiki/wiki/Beast_Tamer) - spawns familiar. The only useful one is Raven, since it blinds enemies, making attack rolls against them easier.

### Background

Choose one from:

- Guild Artisan
- Folk Hero

### Abilities

- 14 STR - boosts damage of some bows. If not needed, invest in CON instead.
- 15+2 DEX (needed for ranged attacks)
- 10 CON
- 8 INT
- 15+1 WIS (needed for spells)
- 8 CHA

### Skills

Prick from priority list:

- Animal Handling - mandatory, nobody else in the party has it
- Stealth

## Level 2

Fighting Style: [Archery](https://bg3.wiki/wiki/Archery).

### Spells

Pick two from priority list:

- [Hunter's Mark](https://bg3.wiki/wiki/Hunter%27s_Mark) - bonus damage per weapon attack, cast as Bonus Action. Buffs your animals, but not party members.
- [Enhance Leap](https://bg3.wiki/wiki/Enhance_Leap)
- [Speak with Animals](https://bg3.wiki/wiki/Speak_with_Animals) - nobody else in the party has it, but it is useless in combat.
- [Longstrider](https://bg3.wiki/wiki/Longstrider) - if nobody else has it.

## Level 3

Subclass: [Beast Master](https://bg3.wiki/wiki/Beast_Master).

Spell: [Ensnaring Strike](https://bg3.wiki/wiki/Ensnaring_Strike).

Available Companions from strongest to weakest:

- [Dire Raven](https://bg3.wiki/wiki/Dire_Raven_Companion) - has extremely effective Blind debuff that synergizes with you and melee allies.
- [Wolf Spider](https://bg3.wiki/wiki/Wolf_Spider_Companion) - shoots web for single-target or massed control. Poisons enemies, which gives disadvantage on Attack Rolls and Ability Checks, which, in turn, synergizes with allied spellcasters.
- [Bear](https://bg3.wiki/wiki/Bear_Companion) - becomes very strong at level 5, can bait enemies on itself.
- [Boar](https://bg3.wiki/wiki/Boar_Companion) - can knock enemies off-feet.
- [Wolf](https://bg3.wiki/wiki/Wolf_Companion) - does not do anything special, no control spells.

## Level 4

Feat: [Sharpshooter: All In](https://bg3.wiki/wiki/Sharpshooter:_All_In).

## Level 5

Feautures:

- Extra Attack
- [Companion's Bond](https://bg3.wiki/wiki/Companion%27s_Bond) - buffs your animals based on your level.

### New Spells

- [Spike Growth​](https://bg3.wiki/wiki/Spike_Growth) - crowd control.

### Replace Spells (Optional)

Replace any unused spell with:

- [Silence](https://bg3.wiki/wiki/Silence) - enemies can walk out of it, but this at least will force them to relocate and waste movement.

## Level 6

Choose additional Favoured Enemy from priority list:

- [Ranger Knight](https://bg3.wiki/wiki/Ranger_Knight) - gives proficiency with Heavy Armour, which is not very useful for high-DEX build, but other options are even worse.

Choose additional Natural Explorer from priority list:

- [Urban Tracker](https://bg3.wiki/wiki/Urban_Tracker) - gives [Sleight of Hand](https://bg3.wiki/wiki/Sleight_of_Hand) proficiency, allowing easy pickpocketing, disarming traps, picking locks.
- [Wasteland Wanderer: Fire](https://bg3.wiki/wiki/Wasteland_Wanderer:_Fire) - gives resistance to fire, which is the most modely used element.

## Level 7

Feature: [Exceptional Training](https://bg3.wiki/wiki/Exceptional_Training) - animals now can Dash, Disengage and Help as a Bonus action.

### Spells

Pick as many as allowed from priority list:

- [Pass Without Trace](https://bg3.wiki/wiki/Pass_Without_Trace) - whole-party stealth boost

## Level 8

Features

- [Land's Stride: Difficult Terrain](https://bg3.wiki/wiki/Land%27s_Stride:_Difficult_Terrain) - negates slowdown from difficult terrain.
- [Land's Stride: Plants](https://bg3.wiki/wiki/Land%27s_Stride:_Plants) - plant-based terrain does not damage you.

Feat: Ability Improvement +2 DEX

## Level 9

Spell: [Plant Growth](https://bg3.wiki/wiki/Plant_Growth) - AoE slowdown. You are immune to it! Combine with Bard's [Hunger of Hadar](https://bg3.wiki/wiki/Hunger_of_Hadar).

Optionally change any unused spell to any Level 3 spell, probably to [Lightning Arrow](https://bg3.wiki/wiki/Lightning_Arrow). Use against wet targets!

## Level 10

Feature: [Hide in Plain Sight](https://bg3.wiki/wiki/Hide_in_Plain_Sight) - save yourself if you are cornered.

Favoured Enemy: pick any.

Natural Explorer: pick any.

## Level 11

- [Bestial Fury](https://bg3.wiki/wiki/Bestial_Fury) - major animals' buff, they gain second attack.

Spells: pick any.

Replace Spell: replace any if you need.

## Level 12

Ranger's Level 12 is pretty useless, since it only gives one feat and Level 4 spellslot. But since Rangers don't have Level 4 spells, this slot is a bit of a waste. Reference builds suggest to multiclass here.

If you don't want to multiclass, pick [Alert feat](https://bg3.wiki/wiki/Alert) (+5 to initiative).

Else, pick one level of War Domain Cleric or Sorcerer. Cleric option gives you an extra attack and some useful cantrips.
